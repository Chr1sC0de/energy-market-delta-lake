## 1 Introduction

## 1.1 Purpose and scope

These are the Wholesale Market Gas Quality Monitoring Procedures (Victoria) ( Procedures ) made under section 91BL of the National Gas Law (NGL) and rule 289B of the National Gas Rules (NGR).

These Procedures provide for the application of the standard gas quality specifications in relation to the Victorian Declared Transmission System (DTS) including all system injection points , DTS monitoring points, and Declared Distribution System (DDS ) transfer monitoring points along with other matters.

These Procedures provide general information about gas quality and how AEMO will respond to short term gas quality excursions outside the standard gas quality specifications .

The off-specification gas injection process, outlined in clause 2.6.1 of these Procedures, seek a critical balance between risks to public safety by the supply of off-specification gas, and risks to public safety associated with curtailment of the injection, subsequent system disruption and re-lights in gas consumer premises. See also the following provisions of the Victorian Gas Safety Act 1997:

- (a) section 32(c) -a 'gas company' must manage and operate each of its facilities to minimise, as far as 'practicable', the hazards and risks to the safety of the public and customers arising from interruptions to the supply of gas and the reinstatement of an interrupted gas supply; and
- (b) section 33 a 'gas company' must ensure that, as far as 'practicable', the gas which it conveys meets the prescribed standards of 'quality'.

Under rule 289(5), AEMO may accept delivery of offspecification gas, such as where, in AEMO's reasonable opinion, acceptance is necessary to ensure safety, security or reliability of the DTS (and the relevant Registered participant has accurately notified AEMO of relevant information under rule 289(4)).

However, AEMO may also refuse to accept delivery or continued delivery of all or some off-specification gas under rule 289(2), for such a period as AEMO may determine.

The NGL and the NGR prevail over these Procedures to the extent of any inconsistency.

These Procedures may only be amended in accordance with Part 15B of the NGR.

## 1.2 Application

These Procedures apply to AEMO and each person to whom they are expressed to apply.

Responsible gas quality monitoring providers must comply with the requirements of these Procedures for their gas quality monitoring systems and update their gas quality monitoring plan to reflect the requirements of these Procedures.

Responsible gas quality monitoring providers must comply with the additional requirements added to these Procedures in version 2.0 in response to the publication of AS 4564:2025 (specification for general-purpose natural gas update) as soon as practicable. A new gas quality monitoring plan to reflect these additional requirements must be submitted to AEMO within 90 business days from the date on which version 2.0 of these Procedures becomes effective or such other date as AEMO may agree with the responsible gas quality monitoring provider .

## 1.3 Legal and regulatory framework

These Procedures have been made under section 91BL of the NGL and rule 289B.

The application of the standard gas quality specifications , as defined in the NGR, in relation to the DTS was developed by incorporating the requirements of the Victorian Gas Safety (Safety Case) Regulations 2018 ( Regulations ) which applies and modifies AS 4564:2025 General-purpose natural gas and natural gas equivalents ( AS 4564 ).

AEMO is required to have these gas quality monitoring procedures to include:

- (a) the application of the standard gas quality specifications in relation to the DTS;
- (b) gas quality monitoring requirements, including;
- (i) monitoring standards; and
- (ii) the use of gas quality monitoring systems and other required gas quality monitoring measures;
- (c) the equipment to be included in gas quality monitoring systems and equipment standards;
- (d) the matters to be addressed in gas quality monitoring plans ;
- (e) the process for making an election or seeking approval or consent from AEMO to become a responsible gas quality monitoring provider ;
- (f) the arrangements for applying for temporary or permanent modifications to gas quality monitoring arrangements ;
- (g) the arrangement for monitoring compliance with gas quality monitoring arrangements ;
- (h) testing of gas quality monitoring systems and the costs of tests; and
- (i) other matters AEMO reasonably considers necessary or desirable to deal with in the gas quality monitoring procedures relation to gas quality monitoring for the DTS or the operation of the Market .

The gas quality monitoring procedures , as required by rule 289B(3), may include information relating to the application of the standard gas quality specifications in a DDS , where that information is available to AEMO.

## 2 Application of the standard gas quality specifications

## 2.1 Introduction

Gas injected into the Victorian Declared Transmission System (DTS) and a Declared Distribution System (DDS) at market injection points must meet the gas quality specifications , which means:

- (a) the standard gas quality specifications ; or
- (b) in the case of a system injection point , a gas quality standard approved by AEMO in respect of that system injection point pursuant to rule 287(1); or
- (c) in the case of a DDS injection point , a gas quality standard approved by the Distributor in respect of that DDS injection point pursuant to rule 287A(1).

AEMO monitors gas that is injected into the DTS to ensure it meets the standard gas quality specifications or the agreed gas quality standard for the DTS , as per (a) and (b) above.

Distributors monitor gas that is injected at DDS injection points into their respective DDS to ensure it meets the standard gas quality specifications or the agreed gas quality standard for the DDS , as per (c) above.

AEMO is provided the gas quality specifications data, as required by rule 289E(7) and 289G(3), by the responsible gas quality monitoring provider for each DDS injection point and publishes this data to the MIBB.

For the avoidance of doubt, AEMO is not required to undertake action in regard to the gas quality specifications at DDS injection points .

## 2.2 Safety

The gas quality specifications parameters for any covered gases must be verified against the limits specified in Table 3 in these Procedures to ensure the gas is safely transported and delivered to customers. Where applicable, a risk-based management approach must be applied, as detailed in clause 2.12 of these Procedures.

## 2.4 Standard gas quality specifications parameter limits

AEMO has determined the application of the following standard gas quality specifications parameters in relation to the DTS on the basis of the informative standard detailed in AS 4564 or a methodology defined in AS 4564.

## 2.4.1 Gas Odourisation

AS 4564 requires gas odourisation to have an odour intensity that is detectable at a level not exceeding 20% LEL.

This requirement is consistent with Regulation 46 of the Gas Safety (Safety Case) Regulations which provides that for the purposes of sections 33(1) and 33(2) of the Gas Safety Act 1997, it is a prescribed standard of quality that all gas must:

- (a) have an odour which is distinctive and unpleasant; and
- (b) have an odour level that is discernible at one-fifth of the lower explosive limit of the gas .

AEMO has determined that the gas odourant specification requirement for the DTS is a blend of 70% THT and 30% TBM injected into the gas stream at a rate of at least 7 mg/m 3 in the gas. AEMO has determined this gas odourisation regime meets the standard gas quality specifications .

This blend is used as the odour is familiar to the community. Other odourant blends and injection rates are acceptable if they also meet the standard gas quality specifications and Regulation requirements.

While odourant injection rates from 7 to 14 mg/m 3 of gas are acceptable, rates of up to 23 mg/m 3 (usually for a limited term associated with 'conditioning' new pipelines) are acceptable, provided th at this rate of injection does not create 'nuisance' leak reporting.

## 2.4.2 Water content

The maximum water content must be determined by a dewpoint of 0  C at the highest MAOP in the relevant transmission system.

## 2.6.1 Overview of off-specification gas injections into the DTS

The off-specification gas injection process outlined in these Procedures seek a critical balance between risks to public safety by the supply of off-specification gas, and risks to public safety associated with curtailment of the injection, subsequent system disruption and re-lights in gas consumer premises. The provisions of the Gas Safety Act 16 as discussed in clause 1.1, covers a 'gas company' and its role in minimising hazards including from gas quality.

Under rule 289(5), AEMO may accept delivery of off-specification gas , such as where, in AEMO's reasonable opinion, acceptance is necessary to ensure safety, security or reliability of the DTS (and the relevant Registered participant has accurately notified AEMO of relevant information under rule 289(4)).

However, AEMO may also refuse to accept delivery or continued delivery of all or some off-specification gas under rule 289(2), for such a period as AEMO may determine.

Blending of gas in the DTS from multiple injection facilities may maintain the gas quality specification while a specific facility injects off-specification gas. This allows time for a DWGM facility operator to undertake corrective action for the facility to resume the injection of gas that meets the gas quality specifications (without requiring AEMO to cease accepting delivery from the facility).

Where off-specification gas conditions become protracted, extreme and/or frequent, or otherwise represent a situation not adequately covered by these Procedures, AEMO (in consultation with the jurisdictional regulator (Energy Safe Victoria)) may implement different risk management strategies. For example, in the event that repeated excursions may compromise gas safety, potential curtailment actions will be evaluated on the level of impact and risk involved.

In the event of off-specification gas flows, injections may be de-scheduled at the next scheduling horizon for the gas day to minimise the overall risk by reducing the quantity of off-specification gas injected.

These Procedures do not cover mitigation of the effects of gas quality within the gas quality specification range.

As such, AEMO has developed an off-specification gas monitoring and notification framework that accounts for this blending of gas within the DTS.

AEMO's notification of off-specification gas is based on measurements made at a gas quality monitoring system at a system injection point.

## 2.6.2 Monitoring off-specification gas

AEMO applies a hierarchy of responses when off-specification gas injections are detected, based on the off-specification gas limits defined in Table 3 and duration of the off-specification gas excursion. The order of responses can be briefly outlined as follows, from requiring least to most action:

- (a) Notify gas quality specification limit: This response level applies to gas that has exceeded the gas quality specification limit. It is a trigger point for Registered participants to be notified and for the gas quality monitoring provider and the applicable DWGM facility operator to take immediate action to bring the given gas quality parameter/s within specification.
- (b) Mitigate: This response level applies to gas that may adversely impact certain parties. It is a trigger point for actions to be taken by:
- (i) The gas quality monitoring provider and the applicable DWGM facility operator to immediately return gas injection to meet the gas quality specification s. The offspecification gas at this level may require AEMO to not accept delivery at the system injection point .
- (ii) Registered participants to undertake corrective action to mitigate the impact of offspecification gas to end users.

<!-- image -->

- (c) Curtail: This response level applies to gas that may cause significant adverse impacts to one or more relevant parties. It is a trigger point for the injection of the gas to not be accepted by AEMO for delivery at the system injection point .
- (i) If curtail limits are exceeded, AEMO will immediately contact the DWGM facility operator to determine the cause for exceeding the curtail limit and discuss progress to return to specification. The actions to be undertaken are detailed in clause 2.6.3.3(f) to (i).

Different off-specification gas conditions may require different responses. The limits include specific excursion duration and a physical limit for a given gas quality specifications parameter which is defined in Table 4. Registered participants , including the responsible gas quality monitoring provider and DWGM facility operators , must notify AEMO as required by rule 289(4) as soon as they become aware that offspecification gas has been delivered, or is likely to be delivered, at a system injection point . In the absence of information to the contrary, AEMO must treat all results received from gas quality monitoring systems as real .

A responsible gas quality monitoring provider at system injection points must submit an incident investigation report to AEMO as soon as practicable, for any gas incident that impacts AEMO's operation of the DTS. The gas incident investigation report should include:

- An incident summary and chronology;
- Impacts to the DTS;
- Corrective actions undertaken;
- Investigations;
- Root cause determination and findings; and
- Future actions.

AEMO also requires a responsible gas quality monitoring provider at a market injection point to submit an incident investigation report to AEMO as soon as practicable, for any gas incident, where covered gas from the market injection point has entered or impacted the operation of the DTS.

A responsible gas quality monitoring provider, when using an alternate test or measurement result must confirm and validate test results (i.e. online, offline and manual backup measurement included) before providing these to AEMO.

## 2.7 Gas quality monitoring requirements

## 2.7.1 Gas quality monitoring standards

AEMO has applied the gas quality monitoring standards, as described in AS 4564, to inform the standards to be used to undertake gas quality monitoring at AEMO monitoring points.

## 2.8 Gas quality monitoring systems

Gas quality monitoring systems are required at (or near) each system injection point, DTS monitoring point and DDS transfer monitoring point that AEMO considers necessary or desirable to enable AEMO to monitor the quality of gas in the DTS or for the calculation of Market settlements.

The purpose of a gas quality monitoring system is to monitor that the gas quality specification parameters of gas injected into the DTS is within the gas quality specifications .

The gas quality monitoring systems use the gas quality monitoring equipment outlined in clause 2.8.2 to monitor the gas quality specifications parameters.

## 2.8.1 Gas quality monitoring points

AEMO requires a gas quality monitoring system at all system injection points . The gas quality monitoring systems are required at, or an appropriate distance from, a system injection point as agreed with AEMO.

In addition to system injection points , AEMO may require gas quality monitoring equipment to be installed at:

- (a) DTS monitoring points ; and
- (b) DDS transfer monitoring points

The Distributor is responsible for approving any gas quality monitoring system at DDS injection point s.

AEMO may request a new gas quality monitoring system to be setup at a DTS monitoring point or DDS transfer monitoring point in order to ensure the Heating Value Allocation Model accurately operates within the gas quality measurement uncertainty limits detailed in clause 2.9.8.

AEMO publishes the monitoring points used to determine heating values and gas composition data source from gas quality monitoring systems in the technical document DWGM Heating Value and Gas Composition Data Sources.

## 2.8.2 Gas quality monitoring system equipment

Test methodologies and equipment available at a DTS monitoring point must take into account the test conditions, required accuracy, and uncertainty of measurement.

A gas quality monitoring system must include a gas chromatograph for the determination of gas composition (including inert gases), heating value, relative density and Wobbe Index as required by rule 289G(1)(a).

Other gas quality monitoring system equipment at a system injection point and DTS monitoring point must, unless AEMO agrees otherwise, include:

- (a) Oxygen analyser;
- (b) Sulphur analyser for hydrogen-sulphide and total sulphur (AEMO may also require mercaptan sulphur);
- (c) Water analyser;
- (d) Hydrocarbon dewpoint measurement equipment (where the hydrocarbon dewpoint is not directly measured the responsible gas quality monitoring provider must provide documentation and supporting extended gas analysis results that specify how the hydrocarbon dewpoint is calculated);
- (e) Flow meter;
- (f) Pressure and temperature indication;
- (g) Odourant concentration monitoring and verification. Dual verification of odourant injection and concentration is required as odourant concentration measurements can be unreliable and provide a false odourant concentration if there is an issue with the injection skid or odourant tanks. The dual verification must use two methodologies and could include but are not limited to the following options:
- (i) The calculated odourant concentration based on the dosing pump stroke rate;
- (ii) Actual odourant flow via an odourant injection skid flow meter;
- (iii) Monitoring of odourant consumption via real time monitoring of weight scales on the odourant tanks to confirm that odourant dosing calculations and skid measurements are correct;
- (iv) Direct odourant monitoring via measurement instrumentation e.g. an odourant gas chromatograph
- (h) Primary gas analyser (e.g. hydrogen analyser) appropriate for the primary gas being injected.

To ensure continuous transmission of gas quality data to AEMO in real time as required by rule 289G(3),

system .

Gas quality data must be secure from either local or remote unauthorised access by suitable electronic access controls. Gas quality data and passwords are confidential information , and each responsible gas quality monitoring provider must ensure they are treated as confidential information .

Instantaneous readings and hourly average readings of measured online monitoring parameters are to be made available to AEMO.

Hourly average readings are to be retrievable by AEMO from the Remote Telemetry Unit (RTU) at the monitoring point for at least 35 days from when measurements were made.

The data requirements for the responsible gas quality monitoring provider to provide data to AEMO are detailed in the Technical Document: 'Gas Metering CTM Data Requirements'.

The clock for a gas quality monitoring system must be referenced to Australian Eastern Standard Time (AEST) with an accuracy of ± 5 secs.

Time must be recorded based on the Julian calendar as a numbered sequence.

## 2.9.8 Gas quality measurement uncertainty and calibration

To demonstrate compliance with the gas quality specifications (including AS 4564), it is necessary to show, at the accepted levels of precision of the test and calculation methods, that the value of a gas quality specifications parameter or a contaminant is not beyond the specified limit. To meet this requirement, the gas quality monitoring system equipment's measurement methods must have an acceptable and sufficiently low uncertainty.

To demonstrate conformance, the responsible gas quality monitoring provider must document within the gas quality monitoring plan, the standard used to measure each gas quality specification parameter and the precision or uncertainty that is achieved with the selected test method standard. Appendix C of AS 4564 contains a list of potentially suitable standard test methods and information on precision of measurement.

The responsible gas quality monitoring provider may propose to use a standard other than those listed in the Appendix C of AS 4564. Such alternative standards and the precision or uncertainty that is achieved with the selected test method must be documented within the gas quality monitoring plan for the approval by AEMO.

The Gas Safety (Safety Case) Regulations require that Wobbe Index and hydrogen sulphide testing is carried out in accordance with methods accredited by NATA or approved by the jurisdictional regulator, ESV.

For calibration, the uncertainty of reference standards used to calibrate the monitoring instruments must be sufficient to ensure the reliable determination of the measured parameter. The preparation of reference standards must be traceable to national standards and must have acceptable certification.

Note that equipment used for both gas quality monitoring and custody metering purposes (e.g., Gas Chromatographs and temperature measurement), the instrumentation must also meet the uncertainty limits as specified in the Wholesale Market Metering Procedures (Victoria) .

## 2.10.7 Sampling and monitoring contaminants originating from biomethane

Where technically feasible, biomethane sampling must be completed using online monitoring. However, for contaminants where online monitoring is not practical, batch sampling for offline testing may be conducted. All batch testing must follow a documented risk assessment and a proposed sampling methodology.

AS 4564 requires that sampling methodology address the following criteria:

- (a) Initial Sampling
- (i) Sampling and contaminant testing frequency must begin at the point of initial biomethane injection into the DTS, and
- (ii) Batch testing must be conducted monthly.
- (b) Frequency Adjustment Criteria - The sampling frequency may only be reduced when the following conditions are met:
- (i) Change sampling frequency to quarterly when a minimum of two consecutive monthly batch testing confirms that contaminants levels are stable and within the limits specified in Table 4; and
- (ii) Further reduce sampling frequency to 6-monthly when a minimum of two consecutive quarterly batch testing confirms that contaminants levels are stable and within the limits specified in Table 4.

If any two consecutive analyses at the reduced frequency show exceedance of contaminant limits, the sampling frequency must immediately revert to the initial frequency. Any future reduction in sampling frequency must follow the criteria outlined above.

- (c) Risk-based Approach to Determine Sampling Requirements

Where pre-injection testing confirms the absence of specific contaminants, those tests may be excluded from the routine test plan. However, due to potential regular changes expected in biomethane source materials:

## 2.11 Monitoring compliance with gas quality monitoring arrangements

Compliance of a gas quality monitoring provider with their gas quality monitoring arrangements is achieved by:

- (a) The monitoring of gas quality monitoring systems by AEMO (see clause 2.11.1), Market Participants and the responsible gas quality monitoring provider (see clause 2.11.2).
- (b) If an issue is identified, testing may be requested to be undertaken by those identified in (a) above (see clause 2.11.3).
- (c) If the testing determines there is an issue with the accuracy of the gas quality monitoring system , this must be promptly investigated and reported by the responsible gas quality monitoring provider to AEMO and Registered participants (see clause 2.11.4).
- (d) If the investigation and report identify compliance issues AEMO may request an audit be undertaken by the responsible gas quality monitoring provider (see clause 2.11.5).

These processes are outlined in the following clauses.

- (c) the communication status to identify issues with the communication link at the gas quality monitoring system ; and
- (d) the information provided by the responsible gas quality monitoring provider and Market Participants including:

The responsible gas quality monitoring provider must, if it becomes aware that the accuracy of a gas quality monitoring system does not meet the requirements of these Procedures, immediately notify AEMO and provide a report to AEMO and impacted Registered participants detailing the issues with the accuracy of the gas quality monitoring system and the plan to fix these issues.

## 2.11.5 Audit requirements

AEMO may by notice require a responsible gas quality monitoring provider to undertake an audit of gas quality monitoring systems for AEMO monitoring points if in AEMO's reasonable opinion a report provided to AEMO under clause 2.11.4, or any information available to AEMO indicates:

- (a) there is a systemic issue identified at a gas quality monitoring system(s) operated by a responsible gas quality monitoring provider after an investigation and report;
- (b) gas quality data (including gas composition data and heating values) provided to AEMO by the responsible gas quality monitoring provider, shows a statistically higher likelihood of gas quality data issues on the basis of a historical, or other reasonable, benchmark; or
- (c) other recurrent issues identified for a gas quality monitoring system , that after an investigation and report, cannot identify and rectify the cause of the issue.

A responsible gas quality monitoring system must arrange for completion of an audit as soon as practicable after notice from AEMO.

The audit must be completed by an independent third party that, in AEMO's reasonable opinion, has the technical expertise to carry out the audit.

The audit must assess the responsible gas quality monitoring provider ' s compliance with the requirements for gas quality monitoring system for AEMO monitoring points in the Rules and the Wholesale Market Procedures .

The responsible gas quality monitoring provider must provide AEMO with a report from the auditor engaged to complete an audit as soon as practicable after completion of the audit.

Costs associated with an audit, including the costs of the auditor, are to be borne by the responsible gas quality monitoring provider .

## 2.12 Risk-based approach to gas quality management

These Procedures outline the principles and application of a risk-based approach to gas quality management as required by standard gas quality specifications ensuring that risks associated with gas composition, natural gas equivalents, and any contaminants are effectively identified, evaluated, and minimised as far as practicable (AFAP).

A risk-based approach must be implemented by the responsible gas quality provider for system injection points in scenarios where:

- (a) gas does not fully conform to the standard gas quality specifications ; or
- (b) the selection of an alternative method and frequency of gas quality monitoring is being considered; or
- (c) multiple new natural gas equivalent sources, including biomethane and hydrogen blends, are being introduced into the DTS.

<!-- image -->

Where a risk-based approach is adopted, the risk assessment including methodology, assumptions, existing controls, actions raised, and outcomes achieved must be documented in the gas quality monitoring plan .

## 2.12.1 Scope of application

The risk-based approach must be applied to assess and manage the following:

- (a) Off-specification gas quality parameters which deviate from the following limits 26 :
- (i) Oxygen: Pressure up to 3,400 kPa -Oxygen concentrations up to 3 mol%;
- (ii) Oxygen: Pressure above 3,400 kPa -Oxygen concentrations to 1 mol%: and
- (iii) Hydrogen: Hydrogen concentrations above 10 vol%; and
- (b) Alternate sampling and monitoring frequency for gas composition and contaminants.

When developing or adjusting a sampling and monitoring frequency, the risk profile and identified hazards associated with specific contaminants must be fully considered

## 2.12.2 Timing of risk assessment

Risk assessments must be conducted by the responsible gas quality monitoring provider at the following stages:

- (a) During the design phase of the interconnection to existing infrastructure;
- (b) Prior to commissioning of the gas quality monitoring system , as a confirmation or review;
- (c) While operating the gas quality monitoring system ;
- (d) A review of monitoring required under clause 2.12.5and
- (e) When any change in the gas supply source has the potential to alter the gas contaminant levels, including changes to biomethane source material or the injection of gas from alternate sources.

The responsible gas quality monitoring provider must engage in formal consultation with relevant regulatory authorities (i.e., Energy Safe Victoria and WorkSafe Victoria) and other affected stakeholders prior to initiating any risk assessment.

## 2.12.3 Inputs to the risk assessment

As a minimum, the risk-based approach must address the following key elements:

- (a) Source of the gas -The source of the gas influences the potential nature and level of gas properties and contaminants;
- (b) Process and quality control -The processes and equipment, including quality controls, used to produce the gas provide key controls for managing gas properties and contaminants. The risk assessment process shall determine the adequacy and effectiveness of these controls in mitigating identified risks;

26 See section 7.2.1 of AS 4564 -2025 -Specification for general purpose natural gas and natural gas equivalent

- (c) Monitoring and testing -The monitoring and testing of gas quality, coupled with suspension protocols, provide additional control measures. The risk assessment process shall determine the extent of monitoring and testing required and the effectiveness of this approach;
- (d) Co-mingling or blending -Where applicable, co-mingling or blending with other gas sources can be used to manage gas properties and contaminants. The risk assessment process shall determine the suitability and effectiveness of this approach; and
- (e) Infrastructure and appliance resilience -The ability of downstream infrastructure and appliances to manage variations in gas composition shall be assessed. The risk assessment process shall determine the suitability and effectiveness of existing infrastructure and appliances in relation to the proposed gas composition.

## 2.12.4 Infrastructure-specific considerations

Application of the standard gas quality specifications must be tailored through infrastructure specific risk-based assessment as follow to incorporate additional requirements:

- (a) Gas transmission infrastructure : utilise the safety management study approach as outlined in AS 2885.6:2018. Any changes shall follow the change management process in AS 2885.1 or AS 2885.3, as applicable;
- (b) Gas distribution infrastructure : apply the formal safety assessment approach as detailed in AS/NZS 4645.1. Any changes shall follow the change management process in AS/NZS 4645.1;
- (c) Consumer piping systems: implement risk controls that ensure continued conformance with AS/NZS 5601.1;
- (d) Gas appliances: implement risk controls that ensure continued conformance with AS 3645 (for Type A appliances) or AS 3814 (for Type B appliances); and
- (e) CNG dispensers : ensure that risk controls maintain conformance with AS 5092.

## 2.12.5 Monitoring and review

The outcome of risk assessment must be used to develop appropriate monitoring regimes, including the selection of parameters to be monitored and the frequency of monitoring.

Risk assessments should be reviewed and updated as follows:

- (a) Periodically, at intervals determined by the risk assessment outcomes;
- (b) When significant changes occur in gas composition, supply chain, or infrastructure; and
- (c) Following any incidents or near-misses related to gas quality.

All risk assessments, monitoring results, and actions taken must be documented and records to be maintained, where applicable, in accordance with regulatory requirements. The effectiveness of implemented risk controls must be subject to regular evaluations, and improvements to be made where necessary.

## 3 Election of Responsible Gas Quality Monitoring Provider

## 3.1 Introduction

This section sets out the requirements for making an election to be a responsible gas quality monitoring provider and applying to cease to be the r esponsible gas quality monitoring provider for the system injection point under rule 289C(2).

## 3.2 Scope

The purpose of this section is to identify and confirm the authorisation of the Registered participant who has elected to be a responsible gas quality monitoring provider or who has applied to cease to be the responsible gas quality monitoring provider for the system injection point under rule 289C.

## 3.3 Process for the election to be a responsible gas quality monitoring provider

The requirements are as follows:

- (a) The Registered participant must download the form ' Election of Responsible Gas Quality Monitoring Provider ' from AEMO's website.
- (b) The form must be fully completed and signed by an authorised representative of the Registered participant .
- (c) The Registered participant must submit the form to AEMO's Support Hub.
- (d) The Registered participant must provide the 'Election of R esponsible Person ' form to AEMO and the DTS SP in the case of a system injection point , or the relevant Distributor in the case of a DDS injection point, as required by the connection agreement for which the connection has a gas quality monitoring system .
- (e) AEMO will inform the Registered participant of its determination in relation to an election to be a responsible gas quality monitoring provider or request to cease to be a responsible gas quality monitoring provider for a system injection point .
- (f) The relevant Distributor must inform the Registered participant of its determination in relation to an election to be a responsible gas quality monitoring provider or request to cease to be a responsible gas quality monitoring provider for a DDS injection point.
- (g) If approved by AEMO, AEMO will also inform the Registered participant of the date from which they are the r esponsible gas quality monitoring provider or from which they will cease to be the r esponsible gas quality monitoring provider for the system injection point .

If approved by a relevant Distributor , the Distributor must notify AEMO of its determination.
