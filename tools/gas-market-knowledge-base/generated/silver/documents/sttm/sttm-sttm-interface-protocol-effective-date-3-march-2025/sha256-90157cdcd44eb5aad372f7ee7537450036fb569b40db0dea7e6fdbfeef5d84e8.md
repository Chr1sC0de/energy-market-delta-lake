## 1. Introduction

## 1.1. Purpose and scope

In accordance with the National Gas Rules (NGR), AEMO must consult with trading participants, STTM facility operators, STTM distributors and allocation agents on changes to the STTM Interface Protocol (SIP) which governs the provision of communications under Part 20 Short Term Trading Market (STTM) Rules of the NGR (see rule 368(1)).

'Communication' is defined as 'any information, notice, request, bid, offer or other submission or communication to be given by AEMO or by any other person under this Part" (Rule 368(6))

The SIP may specify details to be included in a communication, in addition to those specified in Part 20 of the NGR and the STTM Procedures, if those details are reasonably required by AEMO for the purpose for which the communication is to be given, including the validation of information (Rule 368(4)).

All communications made under Part 20 of the NGR, must comply with the requirements of the SIP, including the form, manner and timing by which communications are submitted (unless expressly stated otherwise in Part 20 of the NGR or AEMO permits a communication made to it to be non-compliant with the SIP). Non-compliant communications may be rejected by AEMO (rule 368(5)).

The primary means by which AEMO communicates information to STTM Participants is:

- by publishing reports on the STTM Market Information System (MIS) as defined in the STTM Reports Specification (Artefact); or
- by Message or Transaction Acknowledgements as defined in the STTM Participant Build Pack (Artefact) provided in response to data file submission.

A communication by these means is taken to have been notified to each relevant STTM Participant at the time the report is published on the STTM Market Information System (MIS) or the Message or Transaction Acknowledgment is posted to a location from which it is accessible by the participant in accordance with the STTM Participant Build Pack (Artefact), as applicable.

Some matters communicated by these primary means may also be confirmed using SMS and/or emails, provided that the primary notice will not be invalid if the participant does not receive an SMS or email confirmation.

## 3. STTM Interface Protocol (SIP) Change Process

AEMO may only amend the SIP in accordance rule 368(2).

## 4. STTM Market Notice

Notifications and communication of market and system information between AEMO and STTM Participants is primarily by the MIS as required by the Rules and STTM Procedures.

Notification by a market notice may include follow up email and SMS as the secondary means for communicating information to STTM Participants. The primary market notice published to the MIS is not invalid if the participant fails to receive the notice via email and SMS.

If the MIS is not available, AEMO and STTM Participants must use the backup communication arrangements, which AEMO will specify via market notice, to communicate the information required. The backup communication arrangements may include email, telephone and teleconferencing platforms, or other means, as communicated by AEMO at the time.

## 4.1. Overview of STTM Market Notice

Part 20 of the NGR and the STTM Procedures may require AEMO to notify STTM Participants of events affecting STTM Participants generally or a group of STTM Participants:

- (a) AEMO may issue Market Notices to STTM Participants by posting a Market Notice on the MIS.
- (b) STTM Participants may access Market Notices the public area of the MIS (i.e. the market notice report).

## 4.2. STTM Market Notice -SMS and email functionality

- (a) A STTM Participant may submit the following contact details to AEMO 's Support Hub to be registered to receive Market Notices from AEMO by SMS and/or email:
- (i) one or more mobile telephone numbers of nominated individuals for the receipt of Market Notice SMS notifications; and
- (ii) one or more email address of nominated individuals for the receipt of Market Notice email notifications.
- (b) The STTM Participant must ensure that email and SMS contact details are up to date.
- (c) AEMO will register the email and SMS contact details provided by an STTM Participant under (a) within five business days.

<!-- image -->

- (d) A communication sent by email and SMS is taken to have been notified to the relevant STTM Participant at the time shown in the computer or telephone from which the email or SMS was sent.
- (e) AEMO may publish public Market Notices on the AEMO website.

Note: AEMO may, at its discretion, send notifications regarding issues notified by STTM facility operators to STTM Participants.

## 4.3. Types of STTM Market Notices

A STTM market notice may be used to notify STTM Participants of any of the following:

- (a) scheduling notifications;
- (b) contingency gas notifications; or
- (c) requests for MOS offers.

## 5. Access and security

## 5.1. Establishing and maintaining user accounts and registered contacts details

- (a) A STTM Participant must maintain with AEMO at least two user accounts (if required by the STTM participant to comply with the Rules) to access the Market Information System (MIS), the STTM WebExchanger (SWEX) and the STTM Webexchanger Interface Engine (SWEXIE).
- (b) A STTM participant must nominate one or more individuals to be a registered contact to act on behalf of the STTM participant in dealings with AEMO.
- (c) A STTM participant is responsible for requesting AEMO update any registered contacts detail when they change.
- (d) AEMO will implement reasonable security and verification measures to address the risk of unauthorised access. STTM participants are solely responsible for managing and ensuring the security of their allocated accounts.

## 5.2. STTM Participant systems

Each STTM Participant is responsible for:

- (a) configuring its systems and networks; and
- (b) maintaining firewalls,

to ensure the secure upload and download of data between the STTM participant's systems and STTM market system (SWEX, SWEXIE and MIS).
