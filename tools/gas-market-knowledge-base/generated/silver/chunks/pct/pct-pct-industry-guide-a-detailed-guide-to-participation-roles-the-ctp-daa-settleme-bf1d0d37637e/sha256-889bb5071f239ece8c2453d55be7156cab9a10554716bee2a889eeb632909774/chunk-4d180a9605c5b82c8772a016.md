The capacity auction operates on a pay-as-cleared basis. All winners of a particular product component pay the same clearing price. Any product component for which transportation capacity remains partially unsold in the capacity auction for a gas day has a clearing price of $0 per GJ for that gas day. Note that auction products are facility-specific.
The lowest-priced accepted bid (marginal bid) sets the clearing price for a product component. And the price of an auction product is the sum of prices across its product components (service points, zones, and segments). Bidders pay the clearing price for each product component in the bid. In total, the bidder will pay the aggregate of the clearing prices of the product components in the bid
Tied bids are subject to tie-breaking (see below).
AEMO reports the price and quantity of each auction product purchased to each winning bidder. And each facility operator is notified of the price and quantity of each auction product allocated on their facility (auction products are facility specific). AEMO also publishes the price (and price sensitivity) of all product components following the completion of an auction run. Bid stacks are not published for the DAA.
