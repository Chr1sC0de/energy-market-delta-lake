To ensure continuous transmission of gas quality data to AEMO in real time as required by rule 289G(3), a primary and secondary (back-up) form of communication is required for all gas quality monitoring systems .
The gas quality monitoring system must communicate gas quality data to AEMO at least once every six minutes.
The continuous communication requirement, under rule 289G, is that AEMO must receipt the gas quality data every 6 minutes from the gas quality monitoring system .
