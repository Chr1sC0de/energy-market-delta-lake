If any two consecutive analyses at the reduced frequency show exceedance of contaminant limits, the sampling frequency must immediately revert to the initial frequency. Any future reduction in sampling frequency must follow the criteria outlined above.
- (c) Risk-based Approach to Determine Sampling Requirements
Where pre-injection testing confirms the absence of specific contaminants, those tests may be excluded from the routine test plan. However, due to potential regular changes expected in biomethane source materials:
- (i) the presence of previously excluded contaminants must be periodically reassessed; and

- (ii) if such contaminants are detected at levels exceeding negligible thresholds, they must be incorporated into future testing plans at a frequency outlined in clause (a) above.
When establishing or adjusting the sampling and monitoring frequency:
- (i) the individual risk profile of each contaminant must be assessed, and
- (ii) all known and potential hazards must be considered to ensure the sampling plan remains proportionate to the contamination risk and aligned with the associated risk level.
