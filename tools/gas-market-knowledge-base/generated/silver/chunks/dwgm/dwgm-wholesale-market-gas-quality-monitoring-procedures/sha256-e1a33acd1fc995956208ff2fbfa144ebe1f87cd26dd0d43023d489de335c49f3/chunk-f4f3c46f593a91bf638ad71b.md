The responsible gas quality monitoring provider is required to establish and maintain additional monitoring and controls that are documented in their gas quality monitoring plan to ensure any risks associated with the following are managed and minimised as far as practicable (AFAP).
- (a) Uncontrolled odourant flow into the DTS, which must include consideration of returning to normal operation after plant maintenance or a shutdown (e.g. manual monitoring of odourant
tank, weigh scale readings, etc.). While remote monitoring systems are not mandatory, the monitoring approach -whether it includes remote systems or not -must provide an adequate level of oversight to prevent the uncontrolled flow of odourant into the DTS.
