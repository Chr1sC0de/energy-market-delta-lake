The responsible gas quality monitoring provider must notify AEMO as soon as they become aware of any incorrect or suspected measurement error and their intended actions. In such event, the following re-testing and risk assessment processes will be used:
- (a) Re-testing as soon as possible (typically within 48 hours);
- (b) Comparison to historic test results and background levels;
- (c) Review of other factors (plant operating conditions, testing equipment performance etc.); and
- (d) Arrange further re-testing to confirm that the gas is within the gas quality specification.
The responsible gas quality monitoring provider or the DWGM facility operator (as per the process documented in the gas quality monitoring plan ) must notify AEMO of the outcome of all the above as soon as the results are available. AEMO will work with the responsible gas quality monitoring provider or the DWGM facility operator to balance the risk of losing gas supply versus the risk of injecting offspecification gas.
