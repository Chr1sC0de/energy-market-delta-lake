- (b) Liquid in the gas injected into the DTS including consideration for facilities wet seal compressors will require the implementation of comprehensive liquid mitigation strategies. This may include the provision of a liquids collection low point drain or vessel to monitor the amount of compressor lube oil passing through the primary compressor lube oil removal equipment (e.g. coalescing filters). Any other method proposed by the responsible gas quality monitoring provider must be documented in the gas quality monitoring plan , and supported by adequate evidence that the proposed method minimises all the associated risk AFAP, for review and approval by AEMO.  This would assist in detecting increased amounts of oil carryover due to oil removal equipment overloading, maloperation, or damage, resulting in oil concentrations above the specification limit entering the DTS.
- (c) For all facilities, particularly those with heavy hydrocarbons present that use non-cryogenic separation methods, the inclusion of C9+ tail characterisation and documentation of the HCDP calculation methodology, including ongoing monitoring and review of gas compositions.
AEMO will review the controls within the gas quality monitoring plan to assess whether risks are reduced to AFAP.
