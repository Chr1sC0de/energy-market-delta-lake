The gas quality monitoring system equipment must be capable of measuring the required parameters within the required uncertainty range for values considered ' inspecification' as well as values considered 'out of specification' up until at least the ' curtail l imit' for that parameter. These limits can be found in Table 4 and Table 5.
If the installed equipment cannot cover the range specified in this procedure , the 'curtail' limit for that injection point will be reduced to the measurement limit of the equipment. In this situation, the responsible gas quality monitoring provider must communicate with the DWGM facility operator , ensuring they are made fully aware of the reduced curtailment limit.
The Regulations require that the instruments for testing of Wobbe Index and hydrogen sulphide (or an instrument that is of a class approved by Energy Safe Victoria (ESV)) determine results at least once every six minutes.
Measurement and analysis of all other gas properties and contaminants should be conducted at a frequency that demonstrates conformance with the limits and requirements of this procedure and relevant standards. Therefore, all other instruments should determine results at the same frequency of at least every six minutes or as otherwise approved by AEMO.
