The price of a spread product represents the price differential between two trading locations.
The specification of a spread product requires a convention to be defined for the pricing of a premium market. In setting the pricing convention it is assumed that gas will flow from one trading location (base product) towards a higher priced trading location (premium product). As such, a positive spread product price reflects a notional gas flow from the base product to the premium product.
In the case of Moomba and Wallumbilla, the hubs are physically connected by the SWQP. While flows will change direction depending on supply and demand, long-term forecasts are for gas flows predominately from Moomba to Wallumbilla. The pricing convention for the Moomba-Wallumbilla spread product is therefore based on flows from Moomba to Wallumbilla, and as such:
- MSP trading location (Moomba) is the base product, and
- WAL trading location (Wallumbilla) is the premium product.
