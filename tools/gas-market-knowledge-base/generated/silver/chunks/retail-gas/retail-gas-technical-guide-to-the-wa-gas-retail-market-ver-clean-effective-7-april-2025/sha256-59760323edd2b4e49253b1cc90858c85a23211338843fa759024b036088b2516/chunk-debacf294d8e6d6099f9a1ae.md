The PCF is a factor that defines the representative pressure, elevation, and temperature of the gas at the meter so that a difference between index readings can be converted to a consumption value at standard conditions (Standard Cubic Metres).
Basic meters have a set outlet pressure and the volume measured is corrected for environmental conditions, using a uniform PCF.
For interval meters, the PCF can be calculated dynamically using the information from the meter's sensors. If there are no sensors, then a standardised PCF is used that relates to the outlet pressure of the meter.
The standard conditions currently prescribed in the Procedures are 101.325 kPa (15 degrees Celsius). However, the PCF seeks to correct globally for all respective meters in each gas zone for these standard conditions.
The pressure at the meter changes the higher it is from sea level (101.325 kPa) and is also affected by barometric pressure. As each meter is in a different location, this factor becomes difficult to calculate and therefore some generalisations are made.
