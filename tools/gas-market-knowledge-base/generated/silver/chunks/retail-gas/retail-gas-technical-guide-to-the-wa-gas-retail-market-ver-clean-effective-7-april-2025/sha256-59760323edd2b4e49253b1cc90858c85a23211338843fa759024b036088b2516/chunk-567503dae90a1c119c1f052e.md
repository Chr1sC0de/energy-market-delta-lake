Measuring gas volume and converting the volume measurement to an energy measure involves:
- using meters to measure the flow of gas;
- converting the flow of gas to a standardised volume (which is dependent upon the pressure, temperature and composition of the gas); and
- converting the standardised volume of gas to energy using the applicable heating value, which describes the amount of energy in a standard volume of gas.
In simple terms:
Energy (Joules) = Flow (F) x Pressure Correction Factor (PCF) x Heating Value (HV)
